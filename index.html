Principle 01
戒慎驕矜
Guard Against Arrogance

我在道場時，內心有一個底層的念頭：

我想被看見。

這不是罪。這是人之常情。

可是，當「想被看見」的念頭太強——

我開始找不到，真正的自己在哪裡。

I had this underlying desire — to be seen.
It's not wrong. It's human.
But when the need to be seen became too strong,
I lost track of who I truly was.
Principle 01
戒慎驕矜
Guard Against Arrogance

謙遜，是成功與失敗的融合。

謙遜是寧靜之道，也是情緒的調和器——

讓你不至高傲，不至凌越他人。

一旦驕傲——

在該同意時，也會固執；

失去客觀的標準，拒絕朋友的忠告與他人的指引；

偏離熟悉的路徑，走向偏移，迷失自己。

Humility is the fusion of success and failure — it keeps you balanced.
It is the path of stillness, the regulator of emotion.
Once pride takes hold: you grow stubborn, lose objectivity,
reject good counsel — and drift from the path you once knew.
Principle 04
廣闊的胸襟
A Generous Heart

曾經跟我一起起跑的人，

現在已經在很多地方超越我了。

每次看到他們的成果，

我說不出來我能真正為他們高興。

這一條，我到現在還做得很不夠。

Those who started alongside me have surpassed me in many ways.
When I see their achievements, I can't honestly say I'm truly happy for them.
This is one principle I still fall far short of.
Principle 02
堅守原則
Hold Fast to Principles

疫情後，我換了一份全新的工作，

進入完全陌生的領域，從頭累積硬實力。

人際相處、行銷、公關、電商、AI開發——

花了比別人更多的時間和力氣。

但在這條路上，我從來沒有懷疑過——

修道，是我這輩子一定要做的事。

不論用什麼方式。

After the pandemic I started from scratch in a new field.
It took far more time and effort than others.
But through it all, I never doubted:
This path — it's what I'm here to do. No matter the form.
Principle 02
堅守原則
史記・孔子世家

孔子遷于蔡三歲，楚使人聘孔子。

陳蔡大夫恐，發徒役圍孔子於野。

絕糧三日，從者病，莫能興。

孔子講誦弦歌不衰。

Stranded. Three days without food. Disciples ill.
Confucius kept teaching, reciting, playing music — undiminished.

Principle 03
各居其要，扶圓補缺
Know Your Role, Fill the Gaps

當我看到有人比我更適合站在那個位置，

我會退開，讓他們好好發揮。

這樣的選擇，讓我失去了一些東西。

但道場需要的，

不是一個永遠站在舞台上的人——

而是能夠持續長久運作的人。

When I see someone better suited for a role, I step back.
That choice has cost me things.
But what the practice needs isn't someone always on stage —
it's someone who can keep showing up, year after year.
橋樑 · Bridge
在世界與道場之間
Between the World and the Practice

這幾年我去到外面的世界，

不是因為我想離開。

是因為我想把資源帶回來。

讓外面的世界和道場之間，

有一個橋樑。

These years I went out into the world —
not because I wanted to leave.
Because I wanted to bring resources back.
To build a bridge between the world outside and the practice within.
Principle 06
對自己負責任
Take Responsibility for Yourself

「東方人造罪，念佛求生西方；
西方人造罪，念佛求生何國？」

— 六祖壇經

我有一個毛病：會不自覺地立下沒辦法實現的承諾。

每年的愿力、說好要錄的教學影片……

我總想著「之後再做」。

真正的人才，不把希望寄託於未來或遠方——

有能量，現在就做。狀態差，就好好休息——

但在行事曆留下紀錄，給未來指定時段的自己。

After finding the path, I had a habit: making promises I couldn't keep.
Annual vows. Videos I said I'd record. Always: "I'll do it later."
A person of real substance doesn't defer hope to the future.
If you have energy — act now.
If you're depleted — rest, and block specific time in your calendar.
Principle 10
尊師重道
聽師調遣

在道場你是主人，還是客人？

對於看不慣的地方，

你會怎麼做？

In the sacred space — are you the host, or a guest?
When you see something you don't like — what do you do?
Principle 10
尊師重道
Honor Your Teacher

負責我們區域的上方與我們都是很有緣的，

奉著上天的明命來陪我們走這一條修道路。

而一個真正成熟、有才能的人，

是要靠自己創出一片斐然的成績，

來榮耀他的上方與道場。

Our teachers were brought to us by Heaven's calling —
to walk this path beside us.
A truly mature and capable person creates their own outstanding achievements
to honor their teacher and the sacred community.

Cultivate the Heart

1968 · Single-handed non-stop circumnavigation.
Three sailors. Three radically different approaches.
Who do you think won?

I may be the teacher here.
But I haven't mastered all ten either.
I only know I'm still on the path — just like you.
